# Pre-designed Tagging for 3' Exons



Use this workflow for C-terminal protein tagging with pre-validated gRNAs and optimized repair templates for common model organism genes.

## Choose Your Tagging Strategy

Pythia offers three tagging modes, each optimized for different genomic contexts:

### Exon 3bp mode
Direct tagging of the terminal exon using 3-bp microhomology repeats. Preserves native 3' UTR. Residual repeats add at most 1-4 amino acid scars in the coding sequence (often less).

### Exon 6bp mode
Direct tagging of the terminal exon using 6-bp microhomology repeats. Preserves native 3' UTR. Often achieves higher integration efficiency but residual repeats add at most 2-8 amino acid scars in the coding sequence (often less).

### Intron mode
Tags via intron targeting with terminal exon replacement. Achieves scarless tagging in a synthetic linker but sacrifices native 3' UTR regulatory elements. Requires adding a splice acceptor and polyadenylation sequence.

## Configure Your Design

**Species**: Select your target organism (e.g., *Xenopus tropicalis*, *Mus musculus*, *Homo sapiens*)

**InDelphi Model**: Select the repair prediction model matching your experimental system (e.g., mESC for early embryos, HEK293T for human cell culture)

**Gene**: Enter the gene name or symbol for your target protein (e.g., myh9, acta2, tubb2a)

**Transcript**: Select the specific transcript isoform you wish to tag. For genes with multiple isoforms, choose the isoform relevant to your biological context.
